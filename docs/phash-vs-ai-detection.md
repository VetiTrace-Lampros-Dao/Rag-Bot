# Perceptual Hashing vs. AI Detection

It is a common misconception that perceptual hashing (pHash) is a form of artificial intelligence used to determine whether content is "real" or "AI-generated." This is fundamentally incorrect, and understanding the distinction is vital for accurately interpreting VeriTrace's results.

## What pHash Actually Does
Perceptual hashing is an algorithmic technique designed to detect **near-identical or visually-similar content**. It does this by evaluating the structural layout, low-frequency data, and overall visual composition of an image or video, reducing it to a compact mathematical fingerprint. If two images look similar to the human eye, their pHashes will be mathematically similar.

## What pHash Does NOT Do
pHash does **not** evaluate the authenticity, origin, or generative nature of the media. 
- It cannot tell if an image was painted, photographed, or generated by Midjourney.
- It cannot detect "deepfakes" unless that specific deepfake has been previously registered in the system.
- It is not an AI classification model.

## Summary
VeriTrace uses pHash strictly for **similarity detection and deduplication** against a known registry of files. It is explicitly NOT an AI-generated-vs-real classification tool, and should never be represented as such.
